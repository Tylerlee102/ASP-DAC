\subsection{Runtime and Mapping}

Table~\ref{tab:runtime} reports measured cycle, commit, and simulator wall-time summaries for \texttt{baseline\_no\_recorder}, \texttt{recorder\_present\_disabled}, and \texttt{recorder\_enabled}. Cycle and commit medians are separate from simulator wall-clock measurements.

Table~\ref{tab:mapped-overhead} reports same-target ECP5 place-and-route for the board-scoped baseline and ReplayCapsule designs. The full-core mapped rows use the same \rcMappedTarget\ target, the same Yosys synth\_ecp5 plus nextpnr-ecp5 flow, the same memory size, and the same board-level I/O assumptions. The recorder presence check passes on the mapped ReplayCapsule netlist.

\subsection{Expanded Scaling Package}

The top-conference artifact adds workload scaling, capsule baseline comparisons, buffer-depth sensitivity, runtime scaling by workload length, event-class ablations, recorder-configuration sensitivity, and mapped memory/buffer/configuration scaling. These results are generated from \texttt{results/processed/workload\_scaling.csv}, \texttt{capsule\_baseline\_comparison.csv}, \texttt{capsule\_baseline\_summary.csv}, \texttt{buffer\_sensitivity.csv}, \texttt{buffer\_sensitivity\_summary.csv}, \texttt{runtime\_scaling.csv}, \texttt{runtime\_scaling\_summary.csv}, \texttt{event\_ablation\_scaling.csv}, \texttt{mapped\_scaling.csv}, \texttt{mapped\_scaling\_overhead.csv}, and \texttt{mapped\_scaling\_summary.csv}. Trace-baseline rows that are not directly emitted by the RTL are labeled as estimated in their CSV notes.

The evaluation answers eight scoped questions. First, replay works across the generated benchmark suite in Table~\ref{tab:replay-success}. Second, replay across workload scales is reported in \texttt{workload\_scaling.csv} and Figure~\ref{fig:topconf-replay-scaling}; PASS requires record, replay, final-signature, and event matching. Third, capsule-size scaling and reductions against trace baselines are reported in \texttt{capsule\_baseline\_summary.csv} and Figure~\ref{fig:topconf-capsule-scaling}; estimated baselines remain labeled as estimated. Fourth, buffer-depth sensitivity is reported in \texttt{buffer\_sensitivity\_summary.csv} and Figure~\ref{fig:topconf-buffer-sensitivity}; no buffer depth is called sufficient unless measured rows support it. Fifth, runtime scaling separates architectural cycle and commit counts from simulator wall-clock time in \texttt{runtime\_scaling\_summary.csv} and Figure~\ref{fig:topconf-runtime-scaling}. Sixth, event-class ablations in \texttt{event\_ablation\_scaling.csv} and Figure~\ref{fig:topconf-event-ablation} distinguish replay-critical corruption from diagnostic-only context. Seventh, mapped scaling in \texttt{mapped\_scaling\_overhead.csv} and Figure~\ref{fig:topconf-mapped-scaling} reports only same-target place-and-route PASS comparisons. Eighth, Table~\ref{tab:limitations} states the exact limits.

The mapped scaling data is FPGA recorder/instrumented-wrapper evidence. It is not ASIC area or power evidence, and it is not an autonomous synthesizable replay-consume datapath. Simulator wall time is reported separately from architectural cycle and commit counts.

\subsection{ReplayCapsule-RV v2 Evidence}

The v2 pass adds generated CSVs for workload-failure diagnosis, v2 buffer capacity estimates, v2 capsule-size estimates, replay-consume RTL tests, replay-consume mapping, second-target mapping attempts, and benchmark diversity status. The workload-failure diagnosis classifies all 150 v1 workload-scaling FAIL rows as buffer overflow. The v2 buffer and capsule CSVs are explicitly labeled analytic where they estimate compressed storage from measured v1 rows; they do not convert any unmeasured v2 full-core replay case into a PASS. The replay-consume controller is tested as a synthesizable prototype: valid capsules consume all events, and missing, duplicate, reordered, wrong-MMIO, wrong-interrupt, wrong-hash, and truncated streams are rejected. The mapped replay-consumer row is standalone prototype evidence and is not a full-core autonomous replay claim.
